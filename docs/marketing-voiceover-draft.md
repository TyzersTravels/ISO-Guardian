# ISOGuardian — Marketing Voiceover Script (Draft v1)

**Purpose:** audio narration to lay over the 5 silent Loom recordings (one per persona), edited together into one ~6-minute product tour video. Also suitable for splitting into 5 standalone 60-90s social clips.

**Tone:** calm, confident, SA-English. Short sentences. First-person plural ("we help you..."). Avoid jargon; when ISO terms appear, define them in-line ("a non-conformance — basically a compliance issue you need to track until it's fixed").

**Pacing rule of thumb:** 130-140 words per minute. The timings below assume you read at that pace; adjust by pausing 0.5s between sentences or trimming a beat if you run long.

**Recording tips:** phone mic in a soft-furnished room is fine. Record seated, water within reach. One take per section — multiple short takes edit together cleanly in Descript or CapCut.

> Meet Thabo. He runs a small manufacturing outfit in Springs, Gauteng. Sixty staff, pursuing ISO 9001 for the first time because his biggest customer now requires it.
>
> Thabo's account was provisioned after a short consultation — there is no risky self-serve signup here. His temporary password arrived by email along with his countersigned service agreement.
>
> This is what he sees on first login. The dashboard summarises every compliance metric that matters on a single screen — open non-conformances, upcoming audits, live compliance scores per standard, and his document library.
>

**On-screen:** Thabo's login → temp-password change → dashboard walkthrough → one document upload with auto-numbering visible.

## Persona 2 — Priya, SHEQ manager, power-user flows (1:15 — 2:20)

> Priya has managed SHEQ systems for fifteen years. She has seen every certification platform and most of them slow her down.
>
> This is her workflow on a typical Monday. She raises three non-conformances from the weekend shift — safety gear unsigned, a waste manifest out of sequence, a calibration certificate expired. Each one gets numbered, assigned to an owner, and given a target close-out date.
>
> Because every NCR is mapped to its ISO clause, her compliance score updates in real time. No monthly spreadsheet gymnastics. No end-of-quarter panic.
>
> When the scheduled internal audit arrives, she exports a branded PDF — her company logo as the hero image, ISOGuardian as a subtle footer. Signature blocks on every close-out, ready to hand to her certification body.

**On-screen:** creating 3 NCRs in rapid succession → assigning → compliance score ticking up → PDF export → open PDF to show branded output.

## Notes for recording session

- **Audio only.** Video is already captured by Playwright or Loom; you are reading over silent footage in post.
- **Breathe between sections.** Better to under-read than over-read — Descript can trim silences, cannot manufacture them.
- **If you fluff a line,** say "pickup" and read the line again from the top of that paragraph. In editing you keep the second take.
- **Export format:** 48 kHz WAV or 320 kbps MP3. Descript accepts both.
