### What Works Against You
1. **No brand recognition** — ISOGuardian is unknown. Trust takes time.
2. **B2B sales cycles are slow** — Businesses don't impulse-buy compliance software. Expect 2-8 week decision cycles.
3. **SA market is small** — ~70K ISO-pursuing businesses, but only ~15K are actively looking for software solutions at any given time.
4. **Budget sensitivity** — SA SMEs are cost-conscious. Even R2,000/mo is a decision that goes through the MD.
5. **No testimonials yet** — Social proof is critical in compliance. You have none to start.

### What Works For You
1. **No real SA competitor at this price point** — The market is underserved.
2. **Government tender demand** — Businesses NEED this. It's not a nice-to-have.
3. **Reseller model** — Partners can scale distribution faster than solo founder selling.
4. **Low churn category** — Compliance software is sticky. Once a business is using it for audits, switching cost is high.
5. **Product is genuinely good** — Multi-standard, POPIA-compliant, mobile-responsive, well-built.

## Revenue Model Detail

### Average Revenue Per User (ARPU)
- Most early customers: Starter (R2,000/mo) = R2,000 ARPU
- As you get larger clients: blended ARPU moves to ~R2,500-R3,000/mo
- Reseller clients may negotiate discounts, reducing effective ARPU to ~R1,800

### Churn Assumptions
- Month 1-3: ~8% monthly (early adopters testing, some drop off)
- Month 4-6: ~5% monthly (better-fit customers)
- Month 7-12: ~3% monthly (compliance software is sticky — switching is painful)

### Lifetime Value (LTV)
- At 3% monthly churn: average customer lifetime = 33 months
- LTV = R2,000 x 33 = **R66,000 per customer**
- Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) target: < R6,600 (10:1 LTV:CAC ratio)
- With organic marketing, CAC is effectively R0 (just your time)

## What Will Make or Break Your Growth

### Top 3 Growth Accelerators
1. **Reseller partners** — One active SHEQ consultant with 20 clients can double your revenue overnight. This is your highest-leverage channel. Invest disproportionate time here.
2. **Testimonials and case studies** — Your first 3 clients are marketing gold. Document everything. Video testimonial > written > none.
3. **LinkedIn consistency** — The compound effect of posting 4x/week for 6 months is massive. Most people give up at month 2. Don't.

### Top 3 Growth Killers
1. **Inconsistency** — Posting for 2 weeks then going silent for a month resets your momentum to zero.
2. **Building instead of selling** — You'll be tempted to add features instead of talking to prospects. Resist. The product is good enough. Sell it.
3. **Ignoring follow-up** — 80% of B2B sales happen after the 5th follow-up. Most people stop at 1. When someone completes your assessment, follow up via WhatsApp within 4 hours. Then again in 3 days. Then in a week. Be helpful, not pushy.

## The Honest Bottom Line

**Month 3 realistic expectation:** 3-6 paying customers, R6,000-R12,000 MRR. You're not quitting your day job yet (if you have one), but you have validated product-market fit.

**Month 6:** 12-18 customers, R24,000-R36,000 MRR. This is where it starts to feel real. You can invest in some paid ads and maybe a marketing VA.

**The "system overload" scenario you mentioned?** That's Month 18-24 if you execute consistently. With 50-100 clients, multiple reseller partners, and a proven acquisition engine, you'll need to scale infrastructure and possibly hire. That's a good problem.

**What I'd focus on if I were you (ranked by impact):**

The product is ready. Now it's about sales execution.
