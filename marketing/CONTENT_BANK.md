**L-EDU-01: ISO 9001 in Plain English**
Most people think ISO 9001 is about paperwork. It's not.

It's about having a system that ensures your product or service is consistently good. Every time. For every customer.

In practical terms for SA businesses, ISO 9001 means:
- You document HOW you do things (so it doesn't live in one person's head)
- You track WHEN things go wrong (NCRs) and fix the root cause
- You review your performance regularly (management reviews)
- You can PROVE all of the above to an auditor

That's it. No mystery. No corporate jargon.

If your business delivers a product or service to customers, ISO 9001 makes you better at it — and gives you proof.

**L-EDU-03: What Actually Happens During an ISO Audit**
First-time audit? Here's what to expect (no surprises):

Stage 1 — Document Review (1-2 days)
The auditor reviews your quality manual, procedures, and records. They're checking: "Does this system exist on paper?"

Stage 2 — Implementation Audit (2-5 days)
The auditor walks your floor, interviews staff, checks records. They're asking: "Do people actually follow the system?"

What they're NOT doing:
- Trying to catch you out
- Looking for perfection
- Expecting zero non-conformances

What they ARE doing:
- Verifying your system works
- Identifying improvement opportunities
- Checking you fix problems when they arise

The secret? Auditors WANT you to pass. They're checking for a functioning system, not a flawless one.

**L-EDU-04: Document Control — The Foundation of ISO Compliance**
If I could give one piece of advice to SA businesses starting their ISO journey:

Get your document control right first.

Everything else — NCRs, audits, management reviews — depends on having controlled documents.

What "controlled" means:
- Every document has a unique number
- Every document has a version and approval date
- Only ONE current version exists (old versions are archived)
- Everyone knows where to find the latest version
- Changes are tracked and approved

If your team is emailing documents with "v2_final_UPDATED.docx" — you don't have document control. You have document chaos.

This is the single most common non-conformance in ISO audits. And the easiest to fix.

**L-EDU-05: Root Cause Analysis — Stop Fixing Symptoms**
Your machine broke down. You fixed it. It broke down again.

Your customer complained. You apologised. They complained again.

This is what happens when you fix symptoms instead of root causes.

ISO requires corrective action — which means finding WHY something went wrong, not just fixing WHAT went wrong.

Simple technique: The 5 Whys
1. Why did the product fail? → The seal was worn
2. Why was the seal worn? → It wasn't replaced on schedule
3. Why wasn't it replaced? → No preventive maintenance schedule exists
4. Why no schedule? → No one was assigned responsibility
5. Why no assignment? → We have no equipment management system

Root cause: Missing equipment management system.
Fix: Implement PM schedule with assigned owners.

That's the difference between a plaster and a permanent fix.

**L-EDU-06: Management Reviews — What ISO Actually Requires**
Clause 9.3 of ISO 9001 requires "management review at planned intervals."

In practice, most companies do this quarterly or biannually.

What must be covered (minimum):
- Status of actions from previous reviews
- Changes in external/internal issues
- Customer feedback and complaints
- Process performance and product conformity
- Audit results
- Non-conformance trends
- Opportunities for improvement
- Resource adequacy

What most companies get wrong:
- No formal minutes or records
- The MD doesn't attend
- Actions aren't assigned or followed up
- It becomes a tick-box exercise

A good management review is your strategic compass. It's where leadership decisions meet operational reality.

**L-EDU-10: Internal Audits — Your Secret Weapon**
External audits happen once a year. Internal audits should happen all year.

ISO requires "planned intervals" for internal audits. Most successful companies audit each department quarterly.

Why internal audits matter:
- Find problems BEFORE the external auditor does
- Train your team on what "good" looks like
- Build a culture of continuous improvement
- Generate evidence of a functioning system

Internal audit tip: Don't audit your own department. Cross-audit. Quality audits production. Production audits warehouse. Fresh eyes find more.

**L-EDU-11: What is a Non-Conformance Report (NCR)?**
An NCR is simply: "Something didn't meet our standard."

Types:
- Minor NCR: The system exists but wasn't followed perfectly
- Major NCR: The system doesn't exist or has completely broken down
- Observation: Not a failure, but a risk that could become one

What to include in a good NCR:
1. What happened (description)
2. Where/when it happened
3. Which clause/requirement was not met
4. Root cause (use 5 Whys)
5. Corrective action (fix the root cause)
6. Preventive action (stop it recurring)
7. Evidence of closure

NCRs aren't failures — they're your improvement engine.
